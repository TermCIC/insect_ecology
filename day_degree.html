Degree-Day Concept

by Dr. Chun-I Chiu

Department of Entomology and Plant Pathology, Chiang Mai University

Introduction

The degree-day concept is a method for understanding and predicting insect development based on temperature. It provides a useful framework for ecological and agricultural applications by quantifying heat accumulation above a specific threshold required for insect development.

Key Concepts

1. Development Threshold

  • Lower Development Threshold (Tmin): The minimum temperature below which development stops.
  • Upper Development Threshold (Tmax): The maximum temperature above which development stops or slows (optional for some models).

2. Degree-Day Calculation

Formula:

For a single day:

\[ \text{Degree-days (DD)} = \text{Average Daily Temperature} - T_{\text{min}} \]

If the daily temperature is below \(T_{\text{min}}\), the degree-day is 0.

Using the Simple Average Method:

\[ \text{DD} = \frac{\text{Max Temp} + \text{Min Temp}}{2} - T_{\text{min}} \]

For example, when Max Temp = 25 \( \degree \), Min Temp = 15 \( \degree \), and Tmin = 10 \( \degree \), the degree-day is:

\[ \text{DD} = \frac{25 + 15}{2} - 10 = 10 \, \degree \]

3. Developmental Milestones

Each insect species requires a specific number of accumulated degree-days (thermal constant) to complete a life stage. For example, eggs may hatch after accumulating 100 degree-days.

4. Applications

  • Predicting Development: Forecast when insects will reach specific life stages.
  • Pest Management: Time interventions, such as pesticide applications, to target vulnerable stages.
  • Population Forecasting: Model population dynamics under varying temperature conditions.

Example

For an insect with \( T_{\text{min}} = 10 \, \degree \):

  • Daily temperatures: Max = 25 \( \degree \), Min = 15 \( \degree \).
  • \( \text{DD} = \frac{25 + 15}{2} - 10 = 10 \, \degree \).
  • If 100 DD is required for egg hatching, it would take approximately 10 days under these conditions.

Factors Affecting Accuracy

  • Microclimate conditions (e.g., soil temperature, canopy shading).
  • Variability in temperature within an insect's habitat.

Formula Summary

Degree-Day (DD):

\[ \text{DD} = \frac{\text{Max Temp} + \text{Min Temp}}{2} - T_{\text{min}} \]

Accumulated Degree-Days:

\[ \text{Total DD} = \sum \text{Daily DD} \]
