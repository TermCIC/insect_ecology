Attraction Methods

Light Trap and Pheromone Trap

Light Trap

Light traps use ultraviolet or visible light to attract night-flying insects such as moths, beetles, and mosquitoes. The insects are drawn to the light source and either fall into a collection container or are captured on sticky surfaces.

Example: A light trap placed in agricultural fields to monitor and control nocturnal moth pests like armyworms.

Pheromone Trap

Pheromone traps use synthetic chemical attractants to mimic the natural pheromones of insects. These traps are highly specific and are used to monitor or control target insect populations.

Example: Pheromone traps set up in a fruit orchard to monitor populations of fruit flies or codling moths.

Pros

  • Targets specific insects with high precision.
  • Can be used for pest monitoring or population control.
  • Long spatial range for attraction.

Cons

  • Non-target insects may not be attracted.
  • Effectiveness depends on environmental factors like light or wind.
  • Requires maintenance and replacement of light bulbs or pheromones.
