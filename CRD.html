Completely Randomized Design (CRD)

What is CRD?

Completely Randomized Design (CRD) is a simple experimental design where treatments are randomly assigned to experimental units without any grouping or stratification. This design assumes that all experimental units are homogeneous and equally likely to receive any treatment.

Advantages of CRD

  • Simple to implement and analyze.
  • Efficient when experimental units are homogeneous.
  • Flexibility in the number of treatments and replicates.

Applications of CRD

  • Suitable for laboratory experiments with controlled conditions.
  • Effective when environmental variability is minimal.

Challenges of CRD

  • Less effective when experimental units are heterogeneous.
  • Sensitive to environmental variations, leading to biased results.
