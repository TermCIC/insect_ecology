Introduction to Ecological Networks

by Dr. Chun-I Chiu

Department of Entomology and Plant Pathology, Chiang Mai University

What are Ecological Networks?

Ecological networks are representations of interactions between species in an ecosystem. These networks help us understand the structure, dynamics, and resilience of ecosystems by visualizing relationships such as predation, competition, and mutualism.

Types of Ecological Networks

1. Food Webs

Food webs illustrate who eats whom in an ecosystem, showing the flow of energy and nutrients. They help identify keystone species and trophic relationships.

2. Mutualistic Networks

These networks depict positive interactions, such as those between pollinators and plants or seed dispersers and fruits, which enhance biodiversity and ecosystem functioning.

3. Host-Parasite Networks

Host-parasite networks show the relationships between hosts and their parasites, helping us understand disease dynamics and their impacts on populations.

Key Concepts

  • Nodes: Represent species or groups of species.
  • Links: Represent interactions, such as feeding or mutualistic relationships.
  • Trophic Levels: Categorize species based on their roles in energy transfer (e.g., producers, consumers, decomposers).
  • Network Metrics: Quantify network properties, such as connectance, modularity, and resilience.

Applications of Ecological Networks

  • Biodiversity Conservation: Identifying keystone species critical to ecosystem stability.
  • Invasive Species Management: Understanding how invasive species disrupt native interactions.
  • Climate Change Studies: Analyzing how shifts in temperature and precipitation affect species interactions.
  • Restoration Ecology: Designing interventions to restore degraded ecosystems based on network dynamics.

Visualizing Ecological Networks

Ecological networks can be visualized using graphs, where:

  • Nodes: Represent species.
  • Edges: Represent interactions (e.g., predation, mutualism).

Advanced tools and software, such as R packages (e.g., igraph, bipartite) or Python libraries (e.g., NetworkX), enable detailed analysis and visualization of ecological networks.

Examples of Ecological Networks

  • Coral Reef Ecosystems: Complex food webs involving fish, corals, and algae.
  • Pollination Networks: Bees, butterflies, and birds interacting with flowering plants.
  • Forest Ecosystems: Interactions among herbivores, predators, and plant species.
