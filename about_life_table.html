Introduction to Life Tables

What is a Life Table?

A life table is a summary of the survival and reproductive patterns of a population. It is a fundamental tool in ecology, demography, and population biology to study population dynamics and predict growth or decline.

Components of a Life Table

Symbol Description
x Age or stage of the individual (e.g., days, weeks, years)
Nx Number of individuals surviving at age x
Lx Proportion of the original cohort surviving to age x
Mx Average number of offspring produced by individuals at age x
R0 Net reproductive rate, or the average number of offspring per individual over its lifetime

Key Metrics

1. Net Reproductive Rate (R0)

The average number of offspring an individual is expected to produce over its lifetime. Formula: \( R_0 = \sum (L_x \times M_x) \)

2. Intrinsic Growth Rate (r)

The rate of population increase under ideal conditions. Formula: Solve for \( r \) such that \( \sum (e^{-r \cdot x} \times L_x \times M_x) = 1 \)

3. Generation Time (Tc)

The average age at which reproduction occurs. Formula: \( T_c = \frac{\ln(R_0)}{r} \)

4. Doubling Time (Dt)

The time required for the population to double in size. Formula: \( D_t = \frac{\ln(2)}{r} \)
